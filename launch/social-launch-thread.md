## LinkedIn post

*[Attach: "Same model. Better thinking." linear-vs-canvas comparison image]*

**One long thread is the wrong shape for thinking.**

I've watched myself do this a hundred times: I ask an AI a genuinely good question, get a useful answer, and then bury it under a pile of follow-ups. By the time I want to go back to that fork in the road, the thread has moved on. The good branch is gone. So most of us quietly stop exploring — we take the first answer because the tool makes the alternative expensive.

That's the problem I built Nodea AI to solve.

Nodea is a branching AI chat canvas. Every reply becomes a node you can fork from — your conversation grows as a tree of branches, not one long thread. From any reply you can spin off a new direction without losing the original. You can pan and zoom the whole tree, compare branches side by side, color the nodes that are working, and group everything into Projects so a week of real thinking doesn't scatter across twenty browser tabs.

It runs on Anthropic's Claude models — Haiku 4.5, Sonnet 4.6, and Opus 4.7 — with automatic routing by complexity, so easy branches stay cheap and the hard ones get the strong model. And if you already work in Claude.ai, our Chrome extension reads your existing conversation's branch structure and imports it as a tree you can keep building on.

I'll be honest about where it is: the canvas, Projects, and the Claude import are live now, free during the beta, no credit card. Bring-your-own-keys, plugins, full export, and a project-wide connected canvas are on the roadmap — not shipped yet. I'd rather tell you what's real than oversell.

We had a quiet first launch. This is the louder one. If you've ever wished you could try three answers instead of settling for one, I'd genuinely love your eyes on it.

Stop scrolling. Start branching. → nodea.ai

## Naming + handle note (read before posting)

The name "Nodea" collides in search with the Springer **NoDEA** mathematics journal and a Node.js tool. To keep our results clean and signal that we're an AI product:

- Use **"Nodea AI"** (or **"Nodea.ai"**) as the name in every bio, profile, and the first mention of any post — never the bare word "Nodea" in places search engines and new readers index.
- Where possible, pair the name with a disambiguating term: **"Nodea AI"**, **"branching AI chat canvas"**, or **"Claude"**. Any standalone post title should read clearly as an AI product.
- Keep the link consistent: **nodea.ai**. Same display URL everywhere so the domain accrues recognition.
- Inside a thread, after the first "Nodea AI" mention, shortening to "Nodea" is fine — the disambiguation only needs to happen once per post, up top.
